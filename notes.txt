notes for Important feature graph

1. **Feature Importance**:
    - The Random Forest model assigns an importance score to each feature based on how much it contributes to reducing the impurity (e.g., Gini impurity) in the decision trees.
    - Features with higher importance scores are more influential in making predictions.

2. **Graph Details**:
    - **Y-axis**: Lists the top 20 features (from the dataset) sorted by their importance scores.
    - **X-axis**: Represents the importance scores of these features.
    - **Bars**: The length of each bar corresponds to the importance score of the respective feature.

3. **Purpose**:
    - This graph helps identify which features are most critical for the model's decision-making process.
    - It can guide feature selection, model interpretation, and understanding of the dataset.

4. **Insights**:
    - Features with the longest bars are the most important.
    - If some features have very low importance, they might be candidates for removal to simplify the model.

This visualization is particularly useful for understanding the inner workings of the Random Forest model and for communicating which features are driving predictions.

1. **Model Accuracy Over Epochs**:
    - The x-axis represents the number of epochs (iterations over the entire training dataset).
    - The y-axis represents the accuracy.
    - Two lines are plotted:
      - **Train Accuracy**: Shows how well the model performs on the training data.
      - **Validation Accuracy**: Shows how well the model generalizes to unseen data (validation set).
    - A gap between the two lines may indicate overfitting if the training accuracy is much higher than the validation accuracy.

2. **Model Loss Over Epochs**:
    - The x-axis represents the number of epochs.
    - The y-axis represents the loss (categorical cross-entropy in this case).
    - Two lines are plotted:
      - **Train Loss**: Indicates how well the model is minimizing the error on the training data.
      - **Validation Loss**: Indicates how well the model is minimizing the error on the validation data.
    - A decreasing loss indicates that the model is learning, while an increasing validation loss may indicate overfitting.

These graphs help evaluate the model's learning process and identify issues like underfitting or overfitting.
